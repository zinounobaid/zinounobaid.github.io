Problem

Aircraft structural components need to maintain required strength and stiffness while minimizing weight to improve fuel efficiency. The goal of this project was to reduce the mass of a bracket-like component while keeping stresses within allowable limits and preserving overall stiffness.

Constraints & Requirements

  • Maintain stresses below the material yield strength with a target safety factor.
  • Preserve the original mounting interface and overall envelope of the part.
  • Keep deflection under a specified limit at critical load cases.
  • Use a manufacturable geometry suitable for machining or standard fabrication.

Approach

I started from the baseline CAD geometry, applied the given boundary conditions and loads in ANSYS, and identified regions with low utilization (low stress relative to allowable). I iteratively removed material from low-stress zones and refined fillets and cutouts, re-running the FEA after each change to track the impact on peak stress and deflection. This process continued until I reached a balance between weight reduction and structural performance.

Results

  • Reduced component mass by approximately XX% compared to the baseline design.
  • Maintained a safety factor of at least YY under the worst-case load.
  • Kept maximum deflection within the original allowable limit.

Notes & Future Improvements

With more time, I would explore topology optimization to automatically identify optimal material distribution, validate the design under additional load cases (fatigue, off-axis loads), and compare manufacturability and cost between different design variants.
